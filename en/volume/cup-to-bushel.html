Volume

Volume is the quantity of three-dimensional space enclosed by some closed boundary, for example, the space that a substance (solid, liquid, gas, or plasma) or shape occupies or contains.

Imperial / American measurements

For the imperial system the standard unit of volume is the cubic foot, or cubic inch.

Metric measurements

Volume is often quantified numerically using cubic metres (m3). The metric system also includes the litre (L) as a unit of volume, where one litre is the volume of a 10-centimetre cube.

Cup

The cup is a unit of measurement for volume, used in cooking to measure liquids.

Bushel

A bushel is an imperial and US customary unit of dry volume, equivalent in each of these systems to 4 pecks or 8 gallons.

1 cup = 0.0067 bushel
