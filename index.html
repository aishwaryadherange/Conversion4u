Metric System

The metric system is an internationally agreed decimal system of measurement that was originally based on the mètre des Archives and the kilogramme des Archives introduced by France in 1799.

Other systems of measurement are used around the world, such as the United States and the United Kingdom, this site aims to help people convert units of measurement. The measurement units are categorized into types such as Temperature Conversion, Weight Conversion, Length Conversion, Area Conversion, Volume Conversion, Speed Converion and Time Conversion.

Imperial / American measurements

The system of imperial units or the imperial system, also known as British Imperial, is the traditional system of weights and measures used officially in Great Britain from 1824 until the adoption of the metric system beginning in 1965. The United States Customary System of weights and measures is derived from it.

British Imperial units are now legally defined in metric terms.
